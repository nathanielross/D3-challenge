Health Risks vs. Demographics

Health Risks Facing Particular Demographics in America

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income

There is a positive correlation between risk factors obesity and smoking in relation to poverty and household income. A visible trend shows that poorer states have higher obesity rates and greater rates of smoking. There was a higher rate of healthcare coverage in wealther states, but lower obesity and smoking rates. However, there are some outliers; states such as North Dakota and Iowa have relatively low poverty and high obesity rates.


Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age

The correlations between health risks and age are a little more difficult to see. The median age in most US states is between 36-40 years old. This made the visualizations a little more cluster which makes it more difficult to identify correlations. However,there are some correlations such as higher median age and higher healthcare coverage; lower median age and lower smoking rates; and lower median age and lower obesity rates.
